Lesson 2: How to Make a Stop Motion Film


What is a Stop Motion Film?

Stop motion filming is where objects are moved or manipulated between still (photographed) frames. This creates the effect of movement when the frames are played back.



Stop Motion Filming Techniques

  1. Use aeroplane mode to ensure there are no disruptions when filming.

  2. Choose a sensible time in between shots to move things, a 10-20 second gap is a good rough guide.

  3. Manual settings:
    tbc from Kate

  4. Have all your subject matter close by so you can easily reach them between shots.

  5. Select a playback speed of 5-7 frames per second for the best effect. This means you will need 75-105 shots for a 15 second film, or 150-210 shots for a 30 second film. This technique does require some time but if you use small movements between shots you will soon take up the 75 shot minimum guide for the next task!

  6. You can pause the timer if you need to come up with more ideas half-way through shooting!

  7. Listen to Bob and embrace "mistakes"!

Aim: To produce a relatively short 15-30 second stop motion film that animates your message as a citizen of the universe.
