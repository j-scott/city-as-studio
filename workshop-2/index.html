Workshop 2:
Text and Image - Creating Meaning

Intro:

In this workshop you will explore filmmaking and storytelling though image and text, taking inspiration from artists using newspapers, everyday objects and found online content. You will discover how to create new meanings and connections though creative processes and arts activism to consider what it means to be a citizen of the universe.

The goal of this workshop will be to produce a stop motion film that articulates your message as a "Citizen of the Universe".

Also remember that we don't expect you to be video professionals yet, this workshop remains a chance for you to experiment and learn. So don't be worried if you aren't totally satisfied with your results today.

Lesson 2: How to make a Stop Motion Film


What is a Stop Motion Film?

Stop motion filming is where objects are moved or manipluated between still (photographed) frames. This creates the effect of movement when the frames are played back.

    Pt.I
  1. What sort of art forms are you most interested in? (e.g. film, music, painting, performance...
  2. Can you give some examples of roles or careers that are involved in these art forms? (Think actor, director, musician, choreographer, writer, painter etc)
  3. Where might these people work/exhibit? (For example: artists may exhibit in a gallery, or they may perform in a pop-up exhibition in a shop front.)


    Pt.II

    Now it’s time to find out some more about these art forms, artists or organisations involved. You may want to interview your art teacher (via email or in person/zoom), a local artist you know, or you may want to research more about how to get into the arts (possibly Oxford Brookes Art and Design Foundation course, for example).

  1. What or who have you chosen to research?
  2. What sort of research have you done and where have you got your information from? (E.g. Interview with "X" or Online research using the "X" website)
  3. What have you found out about your chosen artist, arts organisation or future education or career pathway? (Give at least 5 key points.)
  4. Has this research influenced your future plans?
