Storyboarding

What is a Storyboard?

What does a Storyboard do?

  • It forces you to think where you might place the camera for any given shot.
  • It shows how those shots might later be cut together to produce a flowing scene.
  • It’s an effective means of communicating ideas to collaborators.

It’s said that Alfred Hitchcock considered a film done and dusted once he’d completed his storyboard, and that he saw shooting the film itself as a mere technicality.

While this may read like a folk tale, the reality is that Hitchcock rarely rushed to get his camera out on set to capture spontaneous moments. Instead, he preferred to stage his shots well in advance of production.

It’s remarkable to see how closely his finished scenes aligned with his initial conception.

As you embark on your own storyboard in the next task, remember to think ahead about how you might create these actions in real life after - as Hitchcock did...

SMARTPHONE FILMING TECHNIQUES
